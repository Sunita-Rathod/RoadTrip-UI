Blog Post 1

Marine Drive is also known as the Queen's Necklace because, when viewed at night from an elevated point anywhere along the drive, the street lights resemble a string of pearls in a necklace. The promenade is lined with palm trees.

Blog Post 2

Located in the Sahyadri Mountains, Sinhagad Fort is an ancient fortress known for its historical significance and architecture.Sinhagad Fort has played a vital role in India's freedom struggle. Bal Gangadhar Tilak also known as 'The Father of Indian Unrest' , used the fort as a summer retreat. It is here where Mahatma Gandhi after his return from South Africa had a historic meeting with Tilak.

Blog Post 3

On the banks of the Panchganga River, Kolhapur is one of Maharashtra's most attractive tourist destinations. The Mahalaxmi temple is a well-known religious shrine in this city. Besides, travelers are drawn to the area because of the wide range of flavourful cuisines. If you're visiting Kolhapur, you won't want to miss out on experiencing the traditional foods. Kolhapur gets its name from mythology about Kolhasur. Kolhasur was a monster that Goddess Lakshmi killed..

Blog Post 4

Shirdi is a tiny town in the Maharashtra district of Ahmednagar. Shirdi is the birthplace of the highly regarded saint Sai Baba, who has a shrine dedicated to him. Many temples can be found in this ancient location, which you may visit with your families. Many visitors come here to see Sai Baba in great numbers. It is stated that whoever visits Sai Baba here would have all of his wishes granted.
